The Myths of Climate Change

Thursday, September 27th, 2018

Video on Climate Change Phytoremediation and Emission Simulator

One of the biggest misconceptions about Global Warming is that the first world needs to stop emitting so much carbon emissions. Our approach in the Paris Agreement for taxation and punishment of carbon emissions is unpragmatic... Rather, we should propose that corporations that produce a lot of carbon emissions must produce agriculture that neutralizes their carbon emissions. However, due to the geographic location of a lot of corporations and countries, this is in a lot of contexts impossible to do so.

The Myth of Climate Change is that we must reduce output of emissions. The reality is that we need to utilize those emissions for agriculture and feeding the poor.

While, I am very onboard for the reduction of toxic waste in our air... The focus seems to be purely on Greenhouse Emissions as opposed to toxic elements. This is not rational considering the plan of action proposed. To completely disregard phytoremediation in The Paris Agreement is downright careless and reckless.

Phytoremediation of Toxic Emissions is critical for our own health too, not just climate.

We also have never postulated the aspects of astronomy and how Solar Cycles function. Typically, the Sun will go through a "Grand Minimum" and "Grand Maximum", this is when Sun Spots on the external layer of our Star emits more or less heat through Nuclear Fusion.

We are currently in a "Grand Maximum"

However, despite the changes in the dichotomy of our star, we should not dismiss the changes in emissions as having an impact on our climate. The changes in solar cycles are things we should mindful of, as they directly change our climate as well too... Currently, we are seeing abnormal activity that is in causation with the changes in emissions and climate change.

Climate Forcing is in direct causation of the changes in emissions and climate change

Again, despite evidence showing environmental impact. This does not necessarily mean the onus to balance emissions on specific parts of Earth... In fact, this is a great opportunity to have less developed countries become carbon negative real estate with high agricultural output.

Around 80% of humanity live on less than $10 USD a day.

Taking into consideration how many people are living in poverty... The Paris Agreement should be accountable for this with it's propoganda. Rather, instead of seeking to only restrict GHG emissions... We should seek to balance our input and output of all emissions. There is no doubt that any toxic elements should be completely omitted from our atmosphere... However, CO2 is a very critical part of our existence and Earth... It is an essential part of our own bodies. Thus, we need to simply seek to balance it more effectively.

USA is in the forefront of CO2 emissions at > 350k MT

If you notice in the figure provided by Our World in Data... South America and Africa produce little to no CO2 emissions, and, are also home to the poorest people in the world... It would make rational sense to have carbon negative farms built there, to not only counteract climate change, but, to feed the people there as well.

Rodale Institute, while in the early stages, shows proof of Carbon Negative Agriculture

I am huge proponent for ethical clean agriculture practices... Obviously, conventional agriculture uses a lot of chemicals with known harmful effects on our body... The focus when reducing global carbon output should be on reducing toxic elements for human consumption as well.

Utilizing Hemp as a phytoremediation crop is in the best interest of humanity

Plants that are remediators can absorb toxic elements from both the soil and the ground. Considering that Hemp is great as a building material... It would make logical sense to produce hemp to clean our soil and air... And harvest them for building materials as opposed to consumption. Obviously, hemp intended for human consumption should be in environments with less toxins.

Hemp for infrastructure is not only logical, but counteracts GHG output

So to conclude, we need to seriously reconsider how we approach Climate Change and Greenhouse Emissions. The Paris Agreement seems shortsighted if not downright careless. It is in our best interest to put onus on corporations that produce high amounts of emissions to produce agriculture for 3rd world countries. Thus, allowing a prosperous exchange that alleviates the poor from hunger. We should also factor in remediation as well as geographic location of certain countries... Capitalizing on the geographical location of Third World Countries will help enable Carbon Neutral input and output.
