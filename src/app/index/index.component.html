The Myths of Climate Change

Thursday, September 27th, 2018

Video Carbon Simulator

The Myth of Climate Change is that we must reduce output of emissions. The reality is that we need to utilize those emissions for agriculture and feeding the poor.

The two key points I will be discussing are Carbon Sequestration and Phytoremediation... However, I do want to also discuss socioeconomic impact before moving forward with those key points.

Around 80% of humanity live on less than $10 USD a day.

If there is one thing I feel is the worst when it comes to the Paris Agreement. Is it's complete disregard for the third world... If we are going to be discussing reducing carbon emissions... We should obviously factor in that agriculture can play a big role in reducing carbon emissions. The only way a crop can grow is by absorbing CO2.

USA is in the forefront of CO2 emissions at > 350k MT

That doesn't mean we should completely disregard accountability from first world countries when it comes to CO2 emissions. Obviously USA is in the forefront when it comes to cumulative CO2 emissions. They are also the most capable of providing clean agriculture practices to the third world through technology... So one could only expect such a thing should be done.

We should be accountable for Phytoremediation

I am huge proponent for ethical clean agriculture practices... Obviously, conventional agriculture uses a lot of chemicals with known harmful effects on our body... The focus when reducing global carbon output should be on reducing toxic elements for human consumption as well. I feel this is something that is disregarded almost completely in the Paris Agreement, and it is sort of upsetting considering the air quality in first world countries.

Utilizing Hemp as a phytoremediation crop

Plants that are remediators can absorb toxic elements from both the soil and the ground. Considering that Hemp is great as a building material... It would make logical sense to produce hemp to clean our soil and air... And harvest them for building materials as opposed to consumption. Obviously, hemp intended for human consumption should be in environments with less toxins or no toxins.

Hemp for infrastructure

Hemp has been well known as an excellent material for many other resources outside of human consumption. In any case where Hemp is used for non human consumption. It should most likely be from a crop that was intended for Phytoremediation.

Carbon Sequestration is the most practical way to reduce CO2

There has been plenty of evidence pointing towards Carbon Sequestration as the most effective means of reducing CO2 in the atmosphere. It only makes sense to help incentivize future agriculture companies to use Sequestration practices.

~1 Acre of Sequestrated Agriculture mitigates ~0.4 Tonnes of CO2

Considering the cumulative output, we would need a lot of Sequestrated Agriculture in order to create a carbon neutral environment... However, we can only assume as more research is put into this... More effective means will come into effect later.

In conclusion, I feel that the Paris Agreement doesn't factor in some key things such as socioeconomic problems such as poverty and world hunger in the third world. Furthermore, they also seem to never discuss phytoremediation of toxins as well as Carbon Sequestration with Agriculture for CO2 reduction.
